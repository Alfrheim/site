Software Craftsmanship underpins our practices

Continuous Integration and Deployment

Continuos Integration is an key part of software development and release management. It should be in place from the outset and support the team in highlight potential issues/bugs as early in the release pipeline as possible. We work hard to ensure that every feature added is ready to be released into production.

Pair Programming

We value pair programming and believe that it supports our desire to create great quality and bug free software. We also understand that it is not always applicable and apply it programatic to when it provides clear value.

Holistic approach to Testing

We look at testing in a holistic fashion where all type of testing has a place in producing good quality software that adds value. We make a distinction between testing that supports the team (e.g. automated functional testing, unit testing etc.) verses testing that critiques the product (e.g. performance testing, manual explorative functional testing etc.).

TDD the right way

Test Driven Development is a practice that is very hard to master. It not only requires extensive practice but also a deep rooted understudying of good software design. We are mindful that badly written tests can cause more harm than good and well written tests will help keep the system flexible to change.

Shared design responsibility

We believe that the team is responsible for the design of the software and the role of a technical architect is one of an advisor. The technical architect may present different ways of working however the ultimate decision as to how the system is designed, what framework library we use lies with the team. The technical architected should be an active member of the team and influence on the basis of his/her reputation as a developer.

Shared operational responsibility

Release and deployment of an application should lie with the development team as championed by the DevOps movement. We have extensive experience in applying DevOps in highly regulated environment where it was originally thought that DevOps is not possible.

Teams own their internal process

How a team organises it works should be left to the team. If they are not experienced with the Agile processes then a mentor can help them adapt a process to their needs. We have years of experience applying Agile processes such and Scrum and Kanban. We find they are best utilised when tailored to individual teams according to their needs. If project has a number of teams then we have found the following to be invaluable regardless of the process: Daily Team Standup, Daily Across Team Standup and Regular Team and Across Team Retrospectives.
